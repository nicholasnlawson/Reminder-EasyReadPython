Hospital Discharge Letter

Patient Name: John Smith
Date of Birth: 01/01/1970
NHS Number: 123 456 7890
Date of Admission: 01/03/2025
Date of Discharge: 10/03/2025

Diagnosis:
- Pneumonia
- Hypertension
- Type 2 Diabetes

Clinical Summary:
Patient was admitted with shortness of breath and fever. Chest X-ray showed right lower lobe consolidation consistent with pneumonia. Patient was treated with IV antibiotics and showed good clinical improvement. Blood pressure was elevated throughout admission, requiring adjustment of antihypertensive medication.

Medications Prescribed on Discharge:
Amoxicillin [500mg], 1 tablet three times a day for 5 days for pneumonia.
Amlodipine [10mg], 1 tablet once daily in the morning for hypertension.
Metformin [500mg], 1 tablet twice a day with food for diabetes.
Paracetamol [500mg], 1-2 tablets four times a day when required for pain.
Salbutamol Inhaler [100mcg], 2 puffs four times a day when required for shortness of breath.

Dose Changes:
Amlodipine increased from 5mg to 10mg daily.

Medications Started in Hospital Comment:
Amoxicillin started for treatment of pneumonia.

Medications Stopped in Hospital Comment:
None.

Take Home Medications Comment:
7-day supply of all medications provided.

Treatment recommendation (For GP):
Please review blood pressure in 2 weeks and adjust antihypertensive medication as needed.
Consider referral to diabetes clinic for optimization of glycemic control.

Information for the Community Pharmacy:
Patient may need assistance with inhaler technique.

TTO Completed by Ward Pharmacist?: Yes

Medications Authorised by: Dr. Jane Doe
